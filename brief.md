# 🍔 Road Trip Eats — Project Brief

---

## Overview

**Road Trip Eats** is a mobile web application designed for food lovers who want to make the most of every mile on a road trip. Instead of settling for whatever fast food exit appears first, Road Trip Eats helps drivers and passengers discover restaurants that have been featured on popular food and travel television shows — the kind of places that are worth pulling off the highway for.

---

## The Problem

When you're on a road trip, finding a great place to eat is harder than it should be. Generic map apps show you hundreds of options with no context, no story, and no reason to choose one over another. Food lovers know that the best meals on the road come from the hidden gems, the local legends, and the iconic spots that have earned their reputation — many of which have been featured on shows like *Diners, Drive-Ins and Dives*, *Man v. Food*, and *Food Paradise*. But there's no easy way to find those places along your specific route, at the right time of day, that match what you're in the mood for.

---

## The Solution

Road Trip Eats solves this by combining your route, the time of day you'll be passing through an area, and your food preferences to surface TV-famous restaurants that are actually worth the detour. The experience is simple, visual, and built for one-handed use on a phone while you're on the go.

---

## What the Experience Does

1. **You tell it where you're going** — Enter your starting point and destination
2. **It finds the famous spots along your route** — The app identifies TV-featured restaurants within a reasonable distance of your driving corridor
3. **It filters by time of day** — If you'll be passing through an area at 8am, it defaults to showing breakfast spots. Lunchtime shows lunch spots. You get the idea.
4. **You refine by what you're craving** — Filter by cuisine type (burgers, BBQ, Italian, breakfast, seafood, etc.)
5. **You pick your stop** — View restaurant details including what show it was featured on, what dish made it famous, hours, and location
6. **You get there** — A map view shows the restaurant in context of your route so you can easily navigate to it

---

## What It Is Not

- ❌ It is not a full restaurant discovery platform like Yelp
- ❌ It is not a turn-by-turn navigation app
- ❌ It does not use AI to generate suggestions — every restaurant in the app has genuinely been featured on a real food TV show
- ❌ It is not trying to show you every restaurant near your route — it is curated and intentional

---

## The Feel of the Experience

Road Trip Eats should feel like getting a recommendation from a well-traveled foodie friend who has watched every episode of every food show. It's warm, fun, and confident. The UI should be clean and bold — easy to read in a moving car, quick to interact with, and satisfying to use. Think **road trip energy meets food TV excitement**.

---

## Technology Approach

The application will be built as a **mobile web experience**, meaning it runs entirely in a phone's browser with no app download required. It will be built using **Vue.js with Vuetify** — a Material Design component framework — giving it a polished, native app-like feel on any mobile device.

---

## Prototype Scope

For this initial prototype, the app will:

- ✅ Use a **local dataset** of TV-featured restaurants seeded from *Diners, Drive-Ins and Dives* and other shows
- ✅ Support **route-based filtering** along a user-defined driving corridor
- ✅ Support **time-of-day filtering** to surface relevant meal periods
- ✅ Support **cuisine preference filtering**
- ✅ Display a **map view** showing restaurant locations along the route
- ✅ Show **restaurant detail cards** with show info, featured dish, hours, and contact details
